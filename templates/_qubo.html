Transforming from linear program to QUBO

We need to map our linear programs to QUBO (quantum unconstrained binary optimisation) problems. This makes the problems suitable for a quantum annealer such as the D-Wave machine.

We start with a linear program of the form in step one:

$$ \textrm{maximise} \quad \sum_{i=1}^{n}m_ib_i \\ \textrm{subject to} \quad \sum_{i=1}^{n}m_ib_i \leq W_p , \\ \textrm{and} \quad \sum_{i=1}^{n}s_ib_i \leq L $$

Which can be written

$$ \textrm{maximise} \quad \vec{m} \cdot \vec{b} \\ \textrm{subject to} \quad \mathbf{A} \vec{b} \leq \vec{c} $$

where $\vec{b}$ is a vector of $b_i$, $\vec{m}$ is a vector of $m_i$, and $\vec{c}=\left(W_p~L\right)^{\intercal}$. Using slack variables we can make the inequality an equality. This means we now have

$$ \textrm{maximise} \quad \vec{x}^{\intercal} \mathbf{C} \vec{x} \\ \textrm{subject to} \quad \mathbf{B} \vec{x} = \vec{c} $$

$\vec{b}$ extended by slack variables gives $\vec{x}$. $\vec{x}$ contains binary variables, $x_i \in \{0, 1\}$. We can now recast the problem as a quadratic unconstrained binary optimisation problem.

$$ y = \vec{x}^{\intercal} \mathbf{C} \vec{x} + P \left(\mathbf{B} \vec{x} - \vec{c}\right)^{\intercal}\left(\mathbf{B} \vec{x} - \vec{c}\right) \\ y = \vec{x}^{\intercal} \mathbf{C} \vec{x} + \vec{x}^{\intercal} \mathbf{D} \vec{x} \\ y = \vec{x}^{\intercal} \mathbf{Q} \vec{x} + \textrm{constant} $$
